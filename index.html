About

Background

I run Blaine Heffron Consulting. I work with businesses and technical teams that know something is clunky, manual, or slower than it should be, but do not want to hire a big shop just to hear obvious things back from a slide deck.

My background is a little unusual for this kind of work. I earned a PhD in physics from the University of Tennessee, where I worked on the PROSPECT neutrino experiment at Oak Ridge. A lot of that job was extracting signal from noise, building analysis tooling, and getting reliable answers out of messy systems.

That carries over well to consulting. I look at how a system behaves, where it breaks down, and what can be simplified, automated, or rebuilt. Sometimes that means internal AI workflows. Sometimes it means custom software. Sometimes it means a better process with less ceremony around it.

Recent work includes StockForge, Songcraft, Stellar developer tooling, MCP servers for scientific and mathematical workflows, and machine learning for neutrino detector analysis.

I am usually most useful to founder-led companies, operations-heavy teams, and technical groups that have outgrown duct-tape workflows but are not looking for a 10-person consultancy.

How I work

I work in two lanes. One is for founders and small teams who need a workflow fixed. The other is for research and technical work that needs deeper analysis, modeling, or software.

I usually start small. One call, one audit, or one build sprint. That keeps things concrete and makes it obvious pretty quickly whether I can help.

If you run a small business, the offers below are meant to be clear and manageable. If you are dealing with detector analysis, scientific software, custom AI workflows, or hard modeling work, that is a different conversation and I scope it separately.

Get in Touch

If you have a workflow that is wasting time, a software problem that needs a senior hand, or a technical project that keeps getting deferred, send me a note. A short description is enough to start.

The easiest way to begin is to send three things: what is broken, what tools are involved, and whether this is a small-business workflow problem or a more technical R&D-style project. If it looks like a fit, I will suggest the smallest useful starting point.

If you want a faster response, include your rough timeline and budget range. That saves both of us a round trip.
