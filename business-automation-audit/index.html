Business automation audit

Find the workflow worth automating before you build anything.

An automation audit is a short diagnostic for businesses that know manual work is costing them but do not yet know which workflow should be fixed first.

Request an audit Ask a quick question
Good fit

When an audit is the better first move

  • You have several possible automation ideas and no clear priority.
  • A tool or freelancer already failed because the workflow was not mapped first.
  • You need a practical plan before approving a build.
  • You want to know whether the upside is big enough to justify the work.
Approach

How I narrow the list

  1. Inventory the current tools, handoffs, and manual workarounds.
  2. Pick the workflows with visible volume, risk, or revenue impact.
  3. Estimate the value of fixing each workflow with conservative assumptions.
  4. Deliver a build plan, tool recommendation, and measurement approach.
Example

What you get

You get a plain-English workflow map, a ranked automation backlog, a recommended first build, a measurement plan, and notes on what should stay manual.

First numbers to check

  • Workflow volume
  • Time spent per handoff
  • Error or rework rate
  • Revenue risk from delays
